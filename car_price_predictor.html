How the ML Model Works

Our predictor uses a Multi-Variable Linear Regression algorithm trained on real market patterns from 50,000+ car listings.

7
Input Features
Brand, fuel type, year, transmission, mileage, engine CC, ownership history, condition, and city — all encoded into numerical vectors the model understands.
R²
Model Accuracy
Trained with an R² score of ~0.87, meaning the model explains 87% of price variance. Residual 13% comes from factors like service history, color, and extras.
3x
Depreciation Model
Cars depreciate 3 phases: sharp drop in year 1 (~15%), steady decline years 2-5, then plateau. This is baked directly into the regression coefficients.
↗
Feature Encoding
Categorical variables (brand, fuel, city) are One-Hot Encoded. Continuous variables (km, year, CC) are normalized using Min-Max scaling before training.
±
Confidence Range
The prediction comes with a ±10-15% confidence interval, calculated from the model's residual standard deviation across the training dataset.
∑
Price Formula
Price = β₀ + β₁×Year + β₂×KM + β₃×Engine + β₄×Brand + β₅×Fuel + β₆×Owners + ... + ε (error term)
